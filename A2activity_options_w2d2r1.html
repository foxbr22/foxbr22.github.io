Reading Comprehension: Technology Phrasal Verbs

A Conversation About Online Classes

Alex: Hi Sarah! Are you ready for our online study group?

Sarah: Hi Alex! Almost. I just need to *log in* to my email to check for the meeting link.

Alex: Okay. While you're doing that, can you hear me clearly?

Sarah: Actually, can you *turn up* the volume? I can barely hear you.

Alex: Sure, I've increased it. Is that better?

Sarah: Much better, thanks! Oh no, now your video is *breaking up*. Can you move to a better location?

Alex: Sorry about that. Let me try something... Is this better?

Sarah: Perfect! By the way, are you using the library computer today?

Alex: Yes, I am. Why do you ask?

Sarah: Just a reminder - don't forget to *log out* of your account when you're done using the public computer.

Alex: Thanks for the reminder! I always forget that. Oh, before I forget, did you *sign up* for that new online course yet?

Sarah: Not yet! Thanks for reminding me. We need to sign up before the deadline, right?

Alex: Yes, the deadline is tomorrow. Let's do it right after our study session.

Sarah: Sounds good! Now, let's get started with our group work.

Comprehension Questions

1. What does Sarah need to do to check for the meeting link?

2. Why does Sarah ask Alex to turn up the volume?

3. What problem occurs with Alex's video?

4. Where is Alex using a computer?

5. What does Sarah remind Alex to do when he's done using the public computer?

6. What haven't Alex and Sarah done yet regarding the new online course?

7. When is the deadline for signing up for the new online course?

8. What are Alex and Sarah planning to do?

9. Which phrasal verb is used to describe the action of registering for the course?

10. What does Alex say he always forgets to do?
