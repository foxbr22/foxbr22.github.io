Phrasal Verbs

What are Phrasal Verbs?

Phrasal verbs are combinations of words that are used like a single verb. They consist of a verb and one or two particles (usually prepositions or adverbs). The meaning of a phrasal verb is often different from the meanings of its individual words.

Common Uses

Phrasal verbs are very common in everyday English, especially in informal speech and writing. They are used to:

  • Express actions
  • Describe situations
  • Replace more formal single-word verbs

Examples

1. Break up

Common meaning: To end a relationship

Example: Sarah and Tom broke up after dating for two years.

Additional meaning: To have problems with video or audio signal

Example: The video call is breaking up due to poor internet connection.

2. Turn up

Common meaning: To arrive, especially unexpectedly

Example: My old friend turned up at the party last night.

Additional meaning: To increase volume

Example: Can you turn up the volume? I can't hear the TV.

3. Call back

Meaning: To return a phone call

Example: I'll call you back as soon as I finish this meeting.

Remember, the meaning of a phrasal verb often cannot be guessed from its parts. It's best to learn them in context and practice using them in sentences.
