Adjectives, Adverbs, and 'Pretty' Usage

Adjectives and Adverbs

Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe or modify nouns (people, places, things, or ideas).

Examples:
- The tall tree
- A beautiful sunset
- An interesting book

Adverbs

Adverbs are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. They often answer questions like how, when, where, or to what extent.

Examples:
- She sings beautifully
- He arrived late
- The car moves very fast

Understanding 'Pretty'

pretty = beautiful (adjective)

pretty + noun = a pretty little house.

pretty + adjective = very (adverb)

Example: That's pretty good. (That's very good.)

Examples:

As an adjective (meaning "beautiful"):

1. She has a pretty smile.
2. The garden looks pretty in springtime.

As an adverb (meaning "very"):

3. The exam was pretty difficult.
4. I'm pretty sure we've met before.
